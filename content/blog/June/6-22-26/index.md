Monday, June 22nd, 2026

Maybe the whole point is to express and create art. To take in how you take in, and put out how you want to, and how you perceive things. 

What's new? I've been a little bit re-enamored with expressing myself without trying to do something or be something or fake it til you make it. Do exactly what you want to do. It's kind of intoxicating or exhilarating in a way.

I've also been thinking about the idea of "movie" as described by David Thomson in a Sudden Flicker of light. This quote was interesting to me: 

"“Movie,” in contrast, refers not to an individual film but to the mode of cognition, the way of seeing and imagining the world, that the medium has imposed on us. Whether or not we go to the movies, most of us live in what he sometimes calls “the condition of movie,” a state of perpetual fantasy and denial."

I thought the other day that wanting or yearning for things that you see from a state of "movie" or because of "movie" are probably not accurate or fulfilling. Maybe that's obvious to some people. 

How do you enjoy summer as an adult? Especially as an adult in San Francisco where its cold and foggy in the summer? How do you not experience fomo?

And finally, a thought I had today, is the feeling of fomo lying to us, and is it the same feeling I have towards corporate success?
