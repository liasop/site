As you may see, I hit a wall of posting. I was feeling like, increasingly, updating the blog everyday with the pictures that I had taken that day and some words, was a chore. It still felt interesting, I like documenting things and I like that this blog prompts me to take more pictures, but I also feel like its not quite living up to its full potential. There is creativity and expression that is not coming through with the old version. So I've decided to try to keep posting, but in a way that feels more interesting and true and expressive.

I struggle a little bit with writing more, and going more in-depth, becuase I feel very aware that this is public and that feels vunerable. But also, I think thats fine, and I think its worth it to feel more creative to have some risk that maybe at some point in the future I will feel embarassed that this is out there for everyone to see. I feel resistant to this feeling like a journal, but I also want to be true and express myself and curate/write/share interesting things! I guess perhaps I am touching on the fear of being known.

Luckily, Kurt sent me some example bloggers who I really enjoy, and I feel more inspired to just write and share and express. Becuase, whatever!

So, the first topic is, brain space, what happens when we (I) don't work and have unstructured, more attuned, less urgent, and more ambling life. To orient the topic, I was recently talking with a co worker of mine who was asking about what I was doing in my free time since quitting one of my part time jobs. We got onto the topic of (1) being very dedicated to work, it being something that we are very passionate about and spending a lot of time and energy on it (2) the opening up of brain space that happens when you are not working. 

Basically, we were noting a difference between us: she works a lot, and is very dedicated to her work, it is very motiviating to her. She likened it to artistry, and maybe her dedication to having artistic parents to instilled in her the idea that you should be very passionate about the work that you do. I on the other hand, at least currently and especially in the past 3 years or so, feel like it is very important to me to not only do work. That I easily feel tunnel visioned when working a full time job, or going to school full time, and that I so much crave the open-endedness of not working, the open-brainness, the other beautiful things there are to experience and do that are outsite the rat race. And thus the need to do multiple things perhaps, balance things, not just have this one full time desk job. Its easy for me to clock out usually, I hardly every work late.

I don't know exactly when this started or why, but I felt a sharp increase about 3 years ago as mentioned. I remember talking with another friend about why this might have occured, and the thing that felt the most truthful was being introduced to a new world that involved embodiment, meditation, spirituality, etc. But I don't think thats the whole answer, and I don't really know the exact connection between the two.

Another topic that feels connected is a Rumi poem about various "wines" in our life, and to be cautious of the wines, especially the ones that have urgency about "what's needed". Rather to gravitate towards wines that make you "amble about like a camel that has been untied" or something along these lines. I love this line. 

In the past since the rise of online everything due to COVID, I've thought that maybe an antidote to to the bad feelings that go along with working so much was to try to not be on my screen as much as possible. To re enter the real world and fully absorb everything that is happening here. And if I'm not doing that, and I am feeling like I need to relax etc. perhaps looking at the blank wall. I've actually really enjoyed looking at the blank wall and seeing what comes about from doing that.

However I think there are meaningful ways to interact with the screen that are mind-opening, inspiring, creative, educational, etc. The internet isn't all bad! But also there are so many bad things, and so many convieniences right at our finger tips that when I really think about them produce a kind of ache in my body for a time/space when you don't have these things.

One related connection is that I spent some time on long wilderness trips, and there is a very specific and very beautiful thing that happens when you are a couple of weeks into not looking at any screen not having any input from the commotion of the world. A healing of the mind, I think, and spiritual connection with the land and the world. So sometimes I feel like I am craving this, and as a result will be like oh we just need to not look at screens.

Caffeine I think is another interesting factor in all of this, as I think going off caffeine was another contributor to this sharp increase in feeling like I suddenly didn't care so much about work and instead opened my mind to other things. At one point, I was like caffeine is the only way I actually enjoy doing work. However, it also makes me afraid. I thought this was a sign I definetly should not being doing whatever work I was doing- as I was both afraid of it and also didn't want to do it without a stimulant.
